# Reading track: Complete beginner

**Who this is for:** Someone who has never owned a stock, has heard
that "investing" is a thing, and is curious about whether it's
something they should do. Pakistani context, no prior finance
exposure.

**Estimated reading time:** ~90 minutes total, or one page per evening
for a week.

**By the end you will be able to:**
- Explain what investing is and why it can outpace a savings account
- Identify the major instruments available (stocks, mutual funds, ETFs)
- Recognize the basic structure of the Pakistan Stock Exchange
- Decide whether you're ready to open your first account

## The path

### 1. [What is investing?](/docs/01-investing-101/what-is-investing)
Start here. Frames investing as deferred consumption — putting money
to work instead of letting it sit. Skim if you already have a strong
mental model of compounding.

### 2. [Why invest?](/docs/01-investing-101/why-invest)
The Pakistani angle: inflation eats savings-account returns. Focus on
the inflation-vs-return chart and skip the historical-data section if
you're impatient.

### 3. [Risk and return](/docs/01-investing-101/risk-and-return)
The single most important concept. Read carefully. The "key takeaways"
section is what you'll keep coming back to.

### 4. [Stocks](/docs/02-investment-basics/stocks)
Your first concrete instrument. Read the worked example twice.

### 5. [What is the PSX?](/docs/03-pakistan-stock-market/what-is-psx)
The local context. Quick read.

### 6. [Opening an account](/docs/04-getting-started-with-psx/opening-an-account)
The bridge from theory to action. Don't actually open one yet — just
understand what's involved.

### 7. [Your first trade](/docs/04-getting-started-with-psx/first-trade)
The capstone. By the end of this page you'll have a complete mental
model of "what happens when I press buy".
