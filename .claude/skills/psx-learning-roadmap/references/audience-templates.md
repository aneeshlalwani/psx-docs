# Audience templates — the common ones

Have these in your head; the user will often ask for variants of them. When the user asks for a track that resembles one of these, anchor your output on the audience's actual question, not on the category structure.

| Audience | Anchor question they bring | Goldilocks reading length |
|---|---|---|
| Money-management beginner | "I'm not even saving yet. Where do I start?" | 4-6 pages from 00, before any investing topic |
| Complete investing beginner | "What is investing? Should I do it?" | 7-10 pages over 1-2 weeks |
| First-time PSX investor | "I want to buy my first share. How?" | 5-7 pages, focused on 04 + 05 |
| Stock-picker (fundamentals) | "How do I tell if a stock is fairly priced?" | 5-8 pages from 07, plus 02 background |
| Active trader (technical) | "How do I time entries and exits?" | 4-6 pages from 08 + 05 |
| Tax-season refresher | "What do I owe FBR on my PSX gains?" | 3-5 pages from 09 + linked context |
| Shariah-focused investor | "How do I invest halal-y on PSX?" | 5-7 pages from 10 + relevant 02/03 |
| Returning intermediate | "I know stocks. What's PSX-specific?" | Skim 01-02, focus on 03-04-05 + 06-09 |
| Zakat-aware holder | "How do I calculate zakat on my portfolio?" | 2-4 pages, mostly 09 |

## Diagonal paths

The default sidebar reads top-down: 00 → 01 → 02 → … → 11. Most readers don't read that way. A returning trader wants to skip 00-02 entirely. A Shariah-focused investor wants to leap from 03 (PSX overview) directly to 10 (Shariah). A tax refresher reader cares about 09 alone. A fundamentals-driven stock picker wants 02 + 07, skipping 08.

Roadmap output should make these diagonals first-class. Don't be apologetic about skipping categories. State clearly that pages can be safely skipped, and why.
