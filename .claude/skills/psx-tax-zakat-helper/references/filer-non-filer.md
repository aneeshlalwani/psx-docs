# Filer vs. non-filer

The mechanics here are stable; only the numbers move year to year. Treat this file as the canonical scaffolding for any "what does filer mean?" paragraph on the site.

## What to explain

- Pakistan has an **Active Taxpayer List (ATL)** maintained by FBR. The ATL is the formal definition of "filer" status.
- **Filer** = your name appears on the ATL because you've filed your annual income tax return for the relevant tax year.
- **Non-filer** = you don't appear on the ATL.
- Non-filers pay **higher withholding rates** on many transactions: dividends, banking transactions over a threshold, vehicle registration, property purchases, etc. — by design, to push more people to file.
- Becoming a filer involves filing a return for the relevant tax year and paying any tax due. Mark the actual cost and process as TODO — they change.
- A taxpayer's ATL status can lapse if they fail to file in a subsequent year. Mention this; it's a common "I thought I was a filer" trap.

## Practical framing for retail investors

When a tax page touches filer/non-filer, the reader's actual question is usually: *"Should I become a filer? What does it cost me, what does it save me?"* Answer that pragmatically:

1. Compare the filer-vs-non-filer rate differential on the specific tax the page is about (CGT or dividend tax). The non-filer differential is usually significant.
2. Note the **annual cost of filing** (a tax practitioner's fee, plus any tax owed). Mark as TODO.
3. State plainly that for most retail PSX investors, becoming a filer pays for itself within the first dividend cycle. **Do not** state this as advice — frame it as the arithmetic the reader should run for themselves.
