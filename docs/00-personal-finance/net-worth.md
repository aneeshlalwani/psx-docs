# Net Worth

Most people measure their financial health by looking at their bank balance. That is not the right number. The right number is **net worth**: everything you own minus everything you owe, on the same day. It is the only figure that captures both sides of your financial life at once.

## What you'll learn

- The definition and formula for net worth
- What counts as an asset and what counts as a liability
- How to interpret a positive, zero, or negative net worth
- A worked example with PKR amounts

## The formula

```text
Net Worth = Assets − Liabilities
```

- **Assets** are everything you own: cash, money in savings, investments, property, vehicles, and any other belongings of meaningful value.
- **Liabilities** are everything you owe: home loan balance, car loan, credit card debt, money owed to family or friends, and any other obligations.

The arithmetic is deliberately simple. The hard part is being honest with yourself when you list each side.

## Three possible outcomes

There are only three results when you calculate your net worth.

1. **Assets exceed liabilities (positive net worth).** You own more than you owe. This is the goal.
2. **Assets equal liabilities (zero net worth).** Everything you own would be wiped out if you settled all your debts today.
3. **Liabilities exceed assets (negative net worth).** You owe more than you own. Even selling everything would not clear what you owe.

If you land at zero or negative, that is not a moral failing, it is a starting point. Most people in their twenties are at zero or negative because of education loans or vehicle financing, and they climb out over time. The point of calculating it is to know.

## A worked example

Suppose your assets and liabilities look like this:

| Assets | Amount (PKR) |
|---|---|
| Bank savings | 350,000 |
| Brokerage account (PSX shares) | 200,000 |
| Car (current resale value) | 1,200,000 |
| Other (gold, electronics, etc.) | 250,000 |
| **Total assets** | **2,000,000** |

| Liabilities | Amount (PKR) |
|---|---|
| Car loan remaining | 600,000 |
| Credit card balance | 80,000 |
| Personal loan from family | 100,000 |
| **Total liabilities** | **780,000** |

Net worth = 2,000,000 − 780,000 = **PKR 1,220,000**.

This is positive, so the financial position is healthy on paper. The next step is to track this same number every six or twelve months. If it grows, you are heading in the right direction. If it shrinks, something in your earning, saving, or spending needs attention.

<Callout type="tip" title="Update it on the same date each year">
  Pick a date you will not forget, like the start of each year or
  Eid, and update your net worth then. Consistency matters more
  than precision.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- Net worth is the difference between what you own and what you owe.
- A positive net worth means assets exceed liabilities.
- The number is a starting point, not a moral judgment.
- Tracking it once or twice a year tells you whether your finances are improving.
