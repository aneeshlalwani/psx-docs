# Diversification

If you put your entire savings into one stock and that stock goes to zero, your savings go to zero. If you put it into ten different stocks across five industries, even a complete loss on one of them costs you only a fraction of your portfolio. That basic insight, often summarised as "do not put all your eggs in one basket", is **diversification**, and it is the single most powerful risk-management tool available to retail investors.

## What you'll learn

- Why diversification works
- The three axes you can diversify across
- The difference between diversification and de-worsification
- How Pakistani investors can diversify in practice

## Why diversification works

Different assets do not all go up and down together. When the cement industry has a bad year, the IT industry might be doing fine. When PSX is down, gold might be up. Holding multiple assets whose returns are not perfectly correlated reduces the volatility of your overall portfolio without necessarily reducing the expected return.

The mathematical name for this is **uncorrelated returns**. The plain-English version: bad things happen, but they rarely happen to everything at once. A diversified portfolio absorbs the bad year of any one asset because the rest of the portfolio is doing something else.

## The three axes of diversification

You can spread risk across three independent dimensions.

### 1. Asset class

Stocks, bonds, real estate, gold, cash. Each behaves differently in different economic conditions. Stocks tend to do well in growth periods. Bonds and gold often hold up better in downturns. Cash provides liquidity and stability but loses to inflation.

A practical Pakistani portfolio might hold:

- 60 percent in PSX equities (or an equity mutual fund)
- 25 percent in fixed income (T-bills, sukuk, fixed deposits)
- 10 percent in gold
- 5 percent in cash for opportunistic buying

These weights are illustrative, not a recommendation. The right mix depends on age, time horizon, and risk tolerance.

### 2. Industry

Within stocks, spread across industries. A portfolio of five oil and gas stocks is not diversified, even though it has five tickers. A portfolio with one cement, one bank, one pharma, one fertiliser, and one technology company is properly diversified across industries, because a downturn in any one industry hits only a fifth of the portfolio.

### 3. Geography

Pakistani investors are increasingly able to invest abroad. International diversification reduces exposure to Pakistan-specific risks (currency depreciation, political instability) by holding some assets denominated in other currencies and exposed to other economies. Within PSX itself, geographic diversification is limited, but holding companies with significant export business achieves something similar.

## How much diversification is enough?

Research has consistently shown that holding more than a handful of stocks captures most of the diversification benefit. Going from 1 to 10 stocks dramatically reduces volatility. Going from 30 to 100 reduces it only slightly. **About 15 to 20 well-chosen stocks across multiple industries** is widely considered enough for individual diversification within an equity portfolio.

The simplest way to achieve this without picking individual stocks is to invest in an [index-tracking mutual fund](/docs/investment-basics/mutual-funds) or [ETF](/docs/investment-basics/etfs) that mirrors the KSE-100 or KMI-30. A single fund unit gives you diversified exposure to a hundred companies at once.

## De-worsification

There is a point past which adding more positions hurts more than it helps. Holding 200 mediocre stocks because "more is better" is not diversification, it is **de-worsification** (a term Peter Lynch popularised). The goal is enough diversification to manage risk, not so much that you cannot keep track of what you own.

## What diversification does not protect against

Diversification reduces **specific risk** (one company, one industry, one country going wrong). It does **not** reduce:

- Market-wide crashes that take everything down at once
- Inflation eroding the real value of all assets simultaneously
- Currency devaluation hitting all PKR-denominated holdings

For those, the answer is time horizon, asset-class diversification (gold, foreign currency, real assets), and patience.

## Key takeaways

- Diversification reduces volatility by holding assets whose returns are not perfectly correlated.
- Spread across asset classes, industries, and geography.
- About 15 to 20 well-chosen stocks across industries is enough for individual diversification.
- Index funds and ETFs are the simplest way for Pakistani retail investors to diversify.
- Diversification reduces specific risk, not market or inflation risk.
