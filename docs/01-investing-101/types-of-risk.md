# Types of Risk

Risk is not a single thing. When professionals talk about risk, they usually mean one of four specific kinds, each with its own causes and its own mitigations. Knowing which risk you are exposed to helps you decide what to do about it.

## What you'll learn

- The four major types of investment risk
- Concrete Pakistani examples for each
- Why "diversification" addresses some risks and not others
- Pakistan-specific risk factors worth knowing about

## 1. Market risk

**Market risk** (also called **systematic risk**) is the risk that the value of your investment will fall because the entire market is falling. It is the broadest kind of risk and affects nearly every asset at the same time.

Causes include political instability, global economic shocks, interest rate changes, currency devaluation, or war. The Pakistani stock market has fallen sharply during multiple crises over the years. No individual stock pick avoids market risk because when the market falls, almost everything falls with it.

**The only real defence:** time. Market downturns historically recover, but the recovery may take months or years. Investors with long horizons absorb market risk more easily.

## 2. Credit risk

**Credit risk** is the risk that someone you have lent money to will fail to repay. It is most relevant for **bonds** and other debt-like investments, where you are effectively the lender.

If you buy a Pakistani corporate bond, the issuer might run into financial trouble and default on the interest or the principal. Government bonds (Pakistan Investment Bonds, T-bills) carry lower credit risk because the government has more ways to repay than a private company.

**Defence:** stick to high-credit-quality issuers, and diversify across multiple bonds rather than concentrating in one.

## 3. Liquidity risk

**Liquidity risk** is the risk that you will not be able to sell your investment quickly without losing significant value. An asset is "liquid" when you can convert it to cash easily at a fair price.

Examples in Pakistan:

- **Liquid:** large-cap PSX stocks like Lucky Cement, Engro, or HBL. Thousands of shares trade every day.
- **Less liquid:** small-cap PSX stocks with low trading volume. You might wait days to find a buyer at a reasonable price.
- **Illiquid:** real estate, jewellery, certain bonds. Selling can take weeks or months and may require accepting a discount.

**Defence:** keep enough of your wealth in liquid assets so you do not have to sell illiquid holdings during emergencies.

## 4. Inflation risk

**Inflation risk** is the risk that rising prices will erode the real purchasing power of your investment returns. If inflation runs at 11 percent and your money grows at 7 percent, your real wealth is shrinking by 4 percent a year, regardless of how safe the deposit feels.

This is the risk most people underestimate, especially in Pakistan where inflation has been historically high. A "safe" savings account is exposed to this risk every single day.

**Defence:** hold at least some assets that historically grow faster than inflation. Equities, real estate, and certain commodities have historically outpaced inflation over long horizons.

## Pakistan-specific risk factors

Two more risks worth recognising in the Pakistani context.

- **Currency risk.** If the rupee weakens against the dollar, the real value of your PKR savings against international goods (imported machinery, foreign education, dollar-denominated debts) falls. Pakistani inflation often correlates with currency depreciation.
- **Political and regulatory risk.** Changes in tax policy, capital controls, or regulatory frameworks can affect the value of investments. The transition from T+2 to T+1 settlement on PSX in February 2026 is one example of a regulatory change that affects how investments work, even if the underlying assets are unchanged.

## What diversification addresses (and does not)

[Diversification](/docs/investing-101/diversification) reduces some risks effectively and others poorly.

- **Reduces well:** company-specific risk (one stock collapses), industry risk (one sector underperforms), geographic risk if you can invest abroad.
- **Does not reduce:** market risk, inflation risk, currency risk. These hit all assets at once and cannot be diversified away.

## Key takeaways

- Investment risk comes in four main flavours: market, credit, liquidity, inflation.
- "Safe" assets like savings accounts are exposed to inflation risk even when principal is secure.
- Long horizons help absorb market risk but do nothing for liquidity or credit risk.
- Currency and regulatory risks matter especially in the Pakistani context.
- Diversification addresses some risks and not others.
