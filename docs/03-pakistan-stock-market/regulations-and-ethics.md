# Regulations and Ethics

A stock market only works if participants believe the rules are fair. If insiders can systematically front-run public information, if a few players can rig prices, or if listed companies can hide bad news, retail investors stop participating. Pakistan's regulatory framework exists exactly to prevent that. This page covers the rules that protect you, the practices that are explicitly illegal, and what to do if you suspect something is wrong.

## What you'll learn

- The role of SECP and PSX as the two main rule-makers
- Two practices that are illegal and carry serious penalties: insider trading and market manipulation
- Disclosure rules that listed companies must follow
- Ethical investing as a parallel framework
- How to report a suspected violation

## SECP and PSX: the regulatory layers

Two regulators sit on top of every PSX-listed company and every licensed broker.

### SECP (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan)

The **apex regulator** for the Pakistani capital market. SECP writes the rules, supervises PSX itself, licenses brokers and asset management companies, and takes enforcement action when serious violations occur. The legal framework SECP operates under includes the **Securities Act, 2015** and a number of supporting regulations covering specific areas (listing rules, broker conduct, takeovers, mutual funds, and so on).

If you need to verify whether a broker, fund manager, or investment adviser is properly licensed, the SECP website maintains the official Active Taxpayer-style registers for the capital market. Verifying registration before handing over money is a core protection.

### PSX (front-line regulator)

PSX itself acts as a **front-line regulator** under SECP authority. It enforces:

- **Listing requirements** on listed companies (minimum size, minimum profitability, free-float requirements).
- **Ongoing disclosure obligations** (quarterly results, annual reports, material announcements).
- **Trading rules** (circuit breakers, order types, prohibited practices on the trading floor).
- **Maintenance and de-listing rules** when a listed company fails to meet ongoing requirements.

When PSX detects a serious violation, it can suspend trading in a stock, fine the participant, refer the matter to SECP, or in extreme cases de-list the company.

## Two practices that are illegal

Two specific behaviours are explicitly prohibited under Pakistani securities law and carry serious penalties including heavy fines and imprisonment.

### Insider trading

**Insider trading** is the act of trading a company's securities based on **material, non-public information**. "Material" means information that a reasonable investor would consider important when deciding whether to buy or sell. "Non-public" means information that has not been disclosed to the broader market.

Examples of insider trading:

- A company executive who buys shares in their own company after learning about an unannounced profitable contract.
- A relative of a CFO who sells shares before the company publicly announces a missed earnings target.
- An auditor who shares draft financial results with a friend, who then trades on them.

Insider trading is illegal because it gives one party an unfair advantage at the expense of everyone else trading on the public market. SECP investigates and prosecutes these cases.

### Market manipulation

**Market manipulation** covers a range of practices designed to artificially distort security prices. The most common variants include:

- **Pump and dump.** A small group of traders inflate a stock's price using false or misleading information, attract uninformed buyers at the elevated price, then dump their own holdings before the truth comes out. Buyers are left with worthless or near-worthless shares.
- **Wash trading.** Buying and selling the same security between accounts under common control to create the illusion of trading volume. Suggests interest that does not actually exist.
- **Spoofing.** Placing large orders with no intent to execute, just to create a misleading impression of supply or demand, then cancelling before the orders fill.
- **Painting the tape.** Coordinated trades right before market close designed to push the closing price in a particular direction, often to influence index calculations or derivatives values.

All of these are illegal under SECP regulations and can result in fines, prosecution, and imprisonment.

## Disclosure: the foundation of fair markets

Most regulations boil down to one principle: **information that affects share prices should be available to everyone at the same time**. Listed companies are required to disclose:

- **Quarterly and annual financial reports** within fixed deadlines.
- **Material events** (large contracts, leadership changes, regulatory actions, acquisitions, lawsuits) as soon as they occur.
- **Insider trades** by directors, officers, and significant shareholders, so the public can see who is buying and selling.
- **Corporate-action announcements** (dividends, bonus, rights, book closure) with sufficient notice.

When companies fail to disclose properly, that itself is a violation, even if no insider trading occurs. The PSX Data Portal at [dps.psx.com.pk](https://dps.psx.com.pk/) is where most of these disclosures appear in real time.

## Ethical investing

Beyond the law, many investors choose to apply their own ethical frameworks to investing. Two are popular in Pakistan:

- **Shariah-compliant investing** screens out companies with primary activities or financial structures incompatible with Islamic principles. The KMI-30 index and most Pakistani Islamic mutual funds operate under this framework. The [Shariah Investing](/docs/category/shariah-investing) section covers screening and purification rules in detail.
- **ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) investing** screens companies based on environmental impact, social responsibility, and governance practices. The [Ethical and ESG Investing](/docs/investment-basics/ethical-and-esg-investing) page covers this and how it overlaps with Shariah investing.

These frameworks are voluntary. They go beyond the legal minimum to reflect the investor's values.

## How to report a suspected violation

If you suspect insider trading, market manipulation, or any other regulatory violation, two channels are open.

1. **Complaint to PSX** through the official complaint mechanism on the PSX website.
2. **Complaint to SECP** through SECP's complaint portal.

Both regulators take retail investor complaints seriously and have investigated cases triggered by tips from individuals.

## Key takeaways

- SECP is the apex regulator; PSX is the front-line regulator under SECP authority.
- Insider trading (trading on material non-public information) and market manipulation (pump-and-dump, wash trading, spoofing) are illegal and carry serious penalties.
- Listed companies are required to disclose financial results, material events, insider trades, and corporate actions on schedule.
- Ethical investing frameworks like Shariah-compliant and ESG investing go beyond the legal minimum and reflect investor values.
- Suspected violations can be reported to PSX or SECP through their official complaint channels.
