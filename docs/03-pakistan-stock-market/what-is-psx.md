# What Is the PSX?

If you have ever looked at a news ticker showing "KSE-100 up 1.2 percent" and wondered what it actually refers to, this page is for you. The **Pakistan Stock Exchange** (PSX) is the national stock exchange of Pakistan. It is the single regulated marketplace where shares of Pakistani public companies are bought and sold, and the institution behind every headline number you see about the local stock market.

## What you'll learn

- A short history of how PSX came to exist

## A short history

PSX is younger than you might think as a single entity, but the institutions that became PSX have been around for decades.

- **1947:** The Karachi Stock Exchange (KSE) was [established on 18 September 1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Stock_Exchange), shortly after Partition.
- **1970:** A second exchange, the Lahore Stock Exchange (LSE), opened in October 1970.
- **1989:** A third exchange, the Islamabad Stock Exchange (ISE), opened in October 1989.

By 2016, having three separate exchanges in one country was creating market fragmentation and limiting Pakistan's appeal to large strategic investors. The Government of Pakistan therefore consolidated them into a single national exchange: **the Pakistan Stock Exchange**, launched on **11 January 2016** under unified rules and a unified regulatory structure.

Later that year, a 40 percent equity stake in PSX was sold to a Chinese consortium. In June 2017, PSX itself listed publicly, with 20 percent of its equity offered to the general public. PSX is therefore now a publicly traded company on its own exchange.

## What PSX does

On any normal trading day, PSX:

- **Lists companies.** Pakistani public companies that meet the listing requirements can offer their shares for trading, raising capital from a wide pool of investors.
- **Operates the trading platform.** All buy and sell orders flow through the **Karachi Automated Trading System (KATS)**, the electronic order-matching engine that brings buyers and sellers together.
- **Publishes prices and indices.** Live share prices, the KSE-100, KSE-30, KMI-30, and other [PSX indices](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/psx-indices) are calculated and disseminated by the exchange.
- **Acts as a front-line regulator.** PSX enforces listing, disclosure, and conduct rules on the companies and brokers that operate on it, and refers serious violations to the SECP.

PSX is a marketplace, not a trader. It does not buy or sell on its own behalf. It runs the rails so others can.

## How PSX is regulated

PSX operates inside a layered regulatory structure.

- **SECP** (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan) is the apex regulator. It writes the rules and supervises PSX, brokers, listed companies, and investment advisers.
- **PSX itself** acts as a front-line regulator under SECP authority, enforcing trading and listing rules in real time.
- **NCCPL** (National Clearing Company of Pakistan Limited) handles the money side of every trade, guaranteeing settlement.
- **CDC** (Central Depository Company) holds shares electronically on behalf of investors and records every change of ownership.

Together, this four-organisation structure (SECP, PSX, NCCPL, CDC) is what makes Pakistani equity investing possible without each investor having to trust each other directly. The pages on [Market Participants](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/market-participants) and [Regulations and Ethics](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/regulations-and-ethics) cover each of these in more depth.

## Why a national stock exchange matters

PSX is not just a place for traders. It plays four roles for the wider economy.

1. **Capital formation.** Companies that need to grow can raise money from investors instead of relying entirely on bank loans.
2. **Allocation of savings.** Household savings get channelled into productive businesses rather than sitting idle.
3. **Price discovery.** The market sets a transparent, observable price for what each listed company is worth, which is useful for owners, lenders, and the economy at large.
4. **Liquidity.** Investors know they can convert their shares to cash on any trading day, which makes them more willing to invest in the first place.

Without a stock exchange, all of these functions would have to be performed less efficiently by private negotiation, slowing capital formation and making the economy poorer over the long run.

## Key takeaways

- PSX is Pakistan's national stock exchange, formed on 11 January 2016 by merging the Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad exchanges.
- The Karachi exchange itself dates back to 1947, the year of Pakistan's founding.
- PSX runs the trading platform, lists companies, calculates indices, and enforces front-line market rules.
- PSX operates within a four-organisation structure: SECP (apex regulator), PSX (front-line), NCCPL (clearing), and CDC (custody).
- A national exchange supports the broader economy through capital formation, savings allocation, price discovery, and liquidity.
