# CDC Account

When you buy a share on PSX, the share itself is not a piece of paper that arrives in the mail. It exists as an electronic record at the **Central Depository Company of Pakistan**, the institution that holds every PSX-listed share on behalf of every investor in the country. Where exactly your shares are recorded inside CDC determines how safe they are, how much control you have, and what happens if something goes wrong with your broker. This page explains the two kinds of CDC account and which one is right for you.

## What you'll learn

- What CDC is and why it exists
- The difference between a CDC Sub-Account and a CDC Investor Account

## What CDC is

The **Central Depository Company of Pakistan** (CDC) is the national securities depository. Every share traded on PSX is held electronically in CDC's central system. When ownership of a share changes hands through a PSX trade, what physically happens is that CDC updates an electronic record: the seller's account drops by one share, the buyer's account rises by one share. There is no paper certificate.

CDC is regulated jointly by **SECP** and the Government of Pakistan, and exists specifically to provide a trusted intermediary that all market participants (PSX, brokers, investors) can rely on. Without CDC, every transaction would have to involve trust between specific brokers and investors, which is impractical at the scale of millions of trades.

## Two kinds of CDC account

CDC offers two account structures for individual investors. The difference between them determines who actually controls your shares and is one of the most important practical decisions a Pakistani investor makes.

### CDC Sub-Account

A **Sub-Account** is held inside your **broker's** main CDC participant account. You are the beneficial owner of the shares, but the operational interface (placing transfer instructions, viewing balances, executing corporate actions) goes through your broker.

- **Maintained by:** the broker, on your behalf.
- **Operational control:** the broker has practical authority to move shares in and out as part of trading.
- **Best for:** active traders who buy and sell frequently, where the broker's involvement makes settlement seamless.
- **Risk:** if your broker misuses client assets or commits fraud, sub-account holders can in rare cases be exposed.

Most retail investors who open a brokerage account get a Sub-Account by default, because that is what the broker offers as part of the trading account.

### CDC Investor Account (IAS)

A **CDC Investor Account**, also called an **Investor Account Services (IAS)** account, is opened directly with CDC, not through a broker. The shares sit in your own account, under your direct control.

- **Maintained by:** [CDC directly, operated only on the written instructions of the account holder](https://www.cdcpakistan.com/businesses/investor-account-services/).
- **Operational control:** complete. Shares cannot move out of the account without your written transfer instruction.
- **Best for:** long-term holders who do not trade frequently, or any investor who wants the strongest safeguard against broker-level operational risk.
- **Cost:** [a service charge of Rs. 100 plus tax applies for cash, pay order, demand draft, or cheque payments at CDC IAS counters](https://www.cdcpakistan.com/businesses/investor-account-services/services/), with annual maintenance fees per CDC's published schedule.

CDC also provides a range of value-added services for IAS holders: a dedicated web portal, IVR phone access, SMS notifications, eStatements, and eAlerts on every transaction.

## The fraud-protection argument

In rare but real cases, brokers have committed fraud by selling client shares without authorisation. Sub-Account holders are exposed to this risk because the broker has the operational ability to execute trades against the account. Investor Account holders are not, because nothing can move out of the account without their direct written instruction.

This is the single biggest practical reason long-term investors should consider an Investor Account. If you intend to hold a share for years, the convenience of a broker-managed Sub-Account no longer outweighs the additional safety of having direct CDC custody.

## When to use each

A practical rule of thumb most experienced investors converge on:

- **Active trading, short-term holding:** a Sub-Account through your broker is fine and operationally easier.
- **Long-term holding (years to decades):** transfer those holdings to a CDC Investor Account as a fraud-protection measure, even if you keep the broker Sub-Account active for new trades.

A common workflow:

1. Open a Sub-Account through your broker as part of opening a trading account.
2. Buy shares through that Sub-Account.
3. For shares you intend to hold long term, open a CDC Investor Account separately.
4. Periodically transfer long-held positions from the broker Sub-Account into the CDC Investor Account.

This setup gives you the best of both worlds: easy daily trading through the broker, plus direct custody for the wealth you have actually accumulated.

## How to open an Investor Account

Two paths are available.

1. **Online via [CDC Access](https://www.cdcaccess.com.pk/)**. CDC's online platform supports remote account opening with electronic KYC. Documents required typically include CNIC, bank account details, and a recent photograph.
2. **In-person at a CDC office.** The traditional path, with the same document requirements but physical presence and signature.

[Account opening typically completes in 1 to 3 working days](https://www.cdcpakistan.com/businesses/investor-account-services/) once documents are in order, similar to a brokerage account.

After the account is active, you receive credentials for the CDC web portal, where you can view holdings, cash balance, and transaction activity at any time.

<Callout type="warning" title="Verify before transferring">
  When you are ready to transfer shares from your broker Sub-Account to your
  CDC Investor Account, confirm the procedure with both your broker and
  CDC. Mistakes in transfer instructions can cause delays, and in rare
  cases, shares ending up in the wrong account. Take your time on the
  paperwork.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- CDC is Pakistan's central securities depository, where every PSX-listed share is held electronically.
- A CDC Sub-Account is held through your broker; a CDC Investor Account is held directly with CDC under your written-instruction-only control.
- Sub-Accounts are convenient for active trading. Investor Accounts give the strongest protection against broker fraud.
- A common workflow is to use the Sub-Account for day-to-day trading and the Investor Account for long-term holdings.
- Investor Account opening takes 1 to 3 working days and can be done online via CDC Access.
