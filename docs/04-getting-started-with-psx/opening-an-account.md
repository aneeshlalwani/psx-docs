# Opening an Account

If you have ever assumed that opening a stock-trading account in Pakistan is a complicated process reserved for professionals, the last few years should change your mind. As of April 2026, [PSX has been hitting record monthly account openings](https://arynews.tv/psx-hits-record-monthly-account-openings-in-april-2026), driven largely by simplified Sahulat Account procedures and online onboarding. The whole process now takes a few days and a single document for most retail investors. This page walks through what you actually need to do.

## What you'll learn

- The two kinds of trading account available to retail investors
- The documents you need to open each one
- How long the process takes
- The online vs in-person paths
- A short checklist before you submit your application

## Two account types

Every Pakistani retail investor opens one of two account types.

### Sahulat Account (simplified)

The **Sahulat Account** was introduced by SECP and PSX to lower the barrier to entry for first-time retail investors. The defining features:

- **Documentation:** simplified, requiring only a Computerised National Identity Card (CNIC) for KYC.
- **Investment cap:** [SECP raised the Sahulat Account cap from Rs. 1 million to Rs. 3 million](https://www.pakistantruth.com/sahulat-account-investment-limit/) in recent years, so total holdings in a single Sahulat account are limited to that ceiling.
- **Trading restrictions:** only the regular market is available. Leverage products (margin trading, margin financing, stock lending and borrowing, and the negotiated deals market) are not permitted, which keeps Sahulat Account holders away from products that can cause large unexpected losses.
- **Multiple Sahulat accounts:** investors can open separate Sahulat Accounts with different brokers, but only one Sahulat Account per broker.
- **Joint holders:** not allowed. Sahulat Accounts are single-holder only.
- **Conversion:** Sahulat holders can convert to a normal trading account at any time if their needs grow.

Sahulat is the recommended starting point for most beginners.

### Normal trading account

A **normal trading account** has no investment cap and no restriction on which markets you can access. It allows leverage products, futures, and the negotiated deals market. The trade-off is more documentation at account-opening, more compliance overhead, and higher minimum deposits at most brokers.

If you intend to invest more than Rs. 3 million in any single broker relationship, plan to use leverage, or want futures access, open a normal trading account from the start.

## Documents you typically need

For a **Sahulat Account**, the basic requirement is a clear copy of your **CNIC**. Most brokers also ask for:

- A **bank account** in your name (for funding the trading account and receiving sale proceeds).
- A **proof of source of income** (salary slip, business documents, or similar) for AML compliance.
- A **proof of address** (utility bill, bank statement) for some brokers.

For a **normal trading account**, brokers typically ask for all of the above plus:

- More detailed source-of-funds documentation.
- A cancelled cheque from your bank account.
- Sometimes a reference from an existing client or a video KYC.

The exact list varies broker-to-broker. Always check the broker's official requirements before submitting.

## Online vs in-person

Most major Pakistani brokers now offer **fully online onboarding**. The flow is typically:

1. Visit the broker's website or app and start the account-opening process.
2. Upload scans or photos of your CNIC and other documents.
3. Complete a short video call for KYC verification.
4. E-sign the account agreement.
5. Receive your account credentials, usually by email.

If your broker does not offer online onboarding, or you would prefer in-person, the in-person path is similar. Visit the broker's office, fill out the same forms, hand over physical document copies, get them attested where required, and sign the account agreement.

## How long it takes

[Account opening usually completes in 1 to 3 working days](https://www.cdcpakistan.com/businesses/investor-account-services/) once your documents are with the broker, assuming everything is in order. Common reasons for delay:

- Missing or unclear scans of CNIC or supporting documents.
- Mismatched name or address across documents.
- Bank account verification taking longer than expected.

## Funding the account

Once your account is open, you fund it by transferring money from your **personal bank account** into the broker's designated client account. Most brokers also accept demand drafts, pay orders, and (less common now) cheques. The deposit reflects in your trading account on the same day or the next business day.

PSX has noted that the minimum amount to start investing at a registered broker is typically [around Rs. 5,000](https://www.psx.com.pk/psx/resources-and-tools/investors/open-an-account-invest), though the exact minimum varies broker by broker.

## A pre-submission checklist

Before you submit your account opening application, run through this checklist:

- [ ] CNIC is clearly readable, both sides scanned.
- [ ] Bank account is in your name only (or you understand the broker's joint-account rules if it is shared).
- [ ] Source-of-income document is recent (within last 3 months for salary slips).
- [ ] Address on documents matches your CNIC address.
- [ ] You have decided whether to open a Sahulat Account or a normal account.
- [ ] You have read the broker's fee schedule (covered in the next section).

## Key takeaways

- Pakistani retail investors open one of two trading account types: Sahulat (simplified, capped, regular market only) or normal (full access, more documentation).
- Sahulat Accounts require only a CNIC and have an investment cap of Rs. 3 million. Recommended starting point.
- Most brokers offer online onboarding with video KYC. The process typically completes in 1 to 3 working days.
- The minimum to start investing is typically around Rs. 5,000, though it varies by broker.
- The next step after opening an account is opening or activating your CDC sub-account, covered in the next page.
