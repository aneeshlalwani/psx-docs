# Trading Hours

If you have been refreshing your broker app at 2 PM expecting the market to be active and finding it dead, the cause is almost always a quirk of PSX's trading-hours schedule. PSX does not run a single continuous session like some Western exchanges. It has a pre-open, a regular session, a special Friday schedule that splits around Jumma prayers, separate Ramadan timings, and a list of public holidays that you should check before assuming the market is open. This page summarises all of them.

## What you'll learn

- The standard Monday-to-Thursday schedule
- The Friday Jumma split
- The pre-open session and what it actually does
- Ramadan-specific adjustments
- Where to verify holiday closures
- What "off-hours market" means and why most retail investors can ignore it

## Standard schedule (Monday to Thursday)

According to the [PSX trading-hours page](https://www.psx.com.pk/psx/exchange/general/trading-hours) and [TradingHours.com market data](https://www.tradinghours.com/markets/psx):

- **Pre-open session:** **09:15 AM to 09:30 AM** (Pakistan Standard Time).
- **Regular trading session:** **09:32 AM to 03:30 PM**.

PSX operates in the **Asia/Karachi** time zone (PKT, GMT+5).

Total active trading time on a normal day is around six hours, which is shorter than US exchanges and longer than some Asian markets. Most retail orders arrive in the first hour and the last hour of the regular session, which tend to be the most active periods.

## Friday Jumma split

Friday is different. Trading splits across two sessions to accommodate Jumma (Friday) prayers:

- **Morning session:** **09:17 AM to 12:00 PM**.
- **Afternoon session:** **02:32 PM to 04:30 PM**.

Between roughly noon and 2:32 PM, the market is closed for the prayer break. New orders cannot be matched during the break, although you can typically place orders into your broker's queue for execution when the afternoon session opens.

## Pre-open session

The 15-minute pre-open session before regular trading is **not** the same as live trading.

- **What you can do:** place new orders, modify existing orders, or cancel orders.
- **What does not happen:** orders are not matched. No trades execute during this period.
- **Why it exists:** the pre-open session lets buyers and sellers express their intentions before live trading starts. At 09:30 AM, all the orders in the book are processed in a single batch to produce a fair **opening price** for each stock, rather than the chaotic price discovery that would happen if everyone tried to trade at exactly 09:30:00.

For retail investors, the practical implication is that orders placed before 09:30 AM go into the pre-open book and influence the opening price, but do not fill instantly when you press confirm.

## Ramadan timings

PSX adjusts trading hours during Ramadan to accommodate fasting. Per [PSX's Ramadan trading notice](https://pkrevenue.com/psx-announces-trade-timings-for-ramadan-2026/), Ramadan timings typically include an earlier pre-open session (around 09:00 AM) followed by a slightly different order-matching window. The exact Ramadan schedule is published by PSX each year in advance.

If you are trading during Ramadan, check the official PSX schedule before placing time-sensitive orders.

## Holidays

PSX closes for public and stock-exchange holidays. The major closures each year include:

- **Eid-ul-Fitr** (multiple days)
- **Eid-ul-Azha** (multiple days)
- **Pakistan Day** (23 March)
- **Independence Day** (14 August)
- **Ashura** (9th and 10th of Muharram)
- **Quaid-e-Azam Day / Christmas** (25 December)
- **Other federal public holidays** as announced by the Government of Pakistan

The official holiday calendar is published on the [PSX website](https://www.psx.com.pk/) at the start of each year. Always check before placing GTC orders that span a holiday or scheduling time-sensitive trades around festivals.

## After-hours and off-hours markets

PSX has separate after-hours and off-hours mechanisms used primarily for institutional negotiated trades, large block trades, and certain corporate transactions. Most retail investors will never need to engage with these. If your broker's platform shows them, you can usually ignore them unless you have a specific reason to be there.

## A practical summary table

| Day | Pre-open | Regular session(s) |
|---|---|---|
| Monday to Thursday | 09:15 to 09:30 AM | 09:32 AM to 03:30 PM |
| Friday | 09:15 to 09:17 AM | 09:17 AM to 12:00 PM, then 02:32 PM to 04:30 PM |
| Ramadan | adjusted (verify with PSX each year) | adjusted (verify with PSX each year) |
| Public holidays | closed | closed |

All times are PKT (GMT+5).

<Callout type="tip" title="Most volume happens at the open and close">
  PSX trading volume is concentrated in the first hour after the
  open and the last hour before the close. The middle of the day
  is usually quiet. If you are trying to place a large order in a
  less-liquid stock, the open or the close usually offers more
  counterparty depth.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- PSX runs a 15-minute pre-open from 09:15 to 09:30 AM, then regular trading 09:32 AM to 03:30 PM, Monday to Thursday.
- Friday splits around Jumma prayers: 09:17 to 12:00 in the morning, 02:32 to 04:30 in the afternoon.
- The pre-open session is for placing and modifying orders only; no trades execute during it.
- Ramadan has separate timings published each year by PSX.
- Holidays close the market entirely. Check the PSX calendar before scheduling time-sensitive trades.
- After-hours and off-hours markets are mostly for institutional trades; retail investors can usually ignore them.
