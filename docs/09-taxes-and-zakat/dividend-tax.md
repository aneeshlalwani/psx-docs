# Dividend Tax

Every cash dividend declared by a PSX-listed company has tax deducted **before** it lands in your bank account. The deduction is automatic; the company computes it, sends the net amount to your account, and forwards the deducted tax to FBR on your behalf. This page covers how the system works, the differential between filer and non-filer rates (which is the single strongest practical argument for filing), and a worked example.

## What you'll learn

## How withholding works

Pakistani dividend tax is a **withholding tax**, meaning it is collected at source by the paying company before the dividend reaches you. The mechanics:

1. The company's board declares a cash dividend (e.g., PKR 6 per share).
2. On the payment date, the company computes the gross dividend you are owed (number of shares × per-share dividend).
3. The company applies the appropriate withholding tax rate, depending on your filer status (and sometimes your specific situation, such as being a Roshan Digital Account holder).
4. The company sends the net amount (gross minus tax) to your registered bank account.
5. The company forwards the deducted tax to FBR on your behalf.

You do not write a separate cheque or file a separate form for dividend tax. It is fully automated through the company's dividend-payment process.

## "Final tax" or interaction with annual return?

A nuance worth understanding. Dividend tax in Pakistan is sometimes treated as **final tax**: settled at source, with no further reconciliation on the annual return. In other cases (or for some investor categories), the dividend gets included in total income on the annual return and reconciled against the tax slabs.

For most retail investors, the practical implication is small if final tax is in force: the tax is settled at the company level, you do not have to do anything additional. If reconciliation is required, you would include dividends on your annual return and any differential is settled there.

## How to read your annual tax statement

Every Pakistani broker (and the company secretarial registrar) issues an **annual tax statement** at year-end summarising:

- Total gross dividends received during the year, by company.
- Total tax deducted, by company.
- Total net dividends credited.

Save this statement. You will need it for:

1. **Filing your annual income-tax return** (especially if dividend tax is treated as advance tax rather than final tax).
2. **Verifying that withholding rates were applied correctly**.
3. **Computing dividend yield and total return** for portfolio analysis.

If a particular dividend was withheld at the non-filer rate but you should have been on the ATL, you can claim a refund through the IRIS portal at year-end. The administrative effort is non-trivial, which is another reason to make sure you are clearly on the ATL before dividend dates.

## Practical action list

1. **Confirm your filer status** before any major dividend record date.
2. **Save every dividend tax statement** for your records.
3. **Cross-check** the rate applied against your expected rate; flag any discrepancies with the registrar or broker.
4. **For overseas Pakistanis**, consider opening a Roshan Digital Account if eligible to access RDA-specific tax treatment.
5. **At year-end**, reconcile total dividends received with the broker's annual statement and your tax return.
