# Glossary

This glossary collects the terms used across the site in one alphabetical reference. Each entry has a plain-English definition, a short Pakistani-context example, and links to the relevant page or related terms. For deeper coverage of any concept, follow the "See also" link to the corresponding doc page.

### Active Taxpayer List (ATL)

The list maintained and published by FBR naming every individual or entity that has filed an annual income tax return for the relevant tax year. Being on the ATL is the formal definition of "filer" status in Pakistan.

**Example:** A retail investor who filed for tax year 2025 appears on the ATL after FBR's update cycle, qualifying them for filer-rate withholding on dividends and capital gains.

**See also:** [Filer](#filer) · [Non-filer](#non-filer) · [Filer vs Non-Filer](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/filer-vs-non-filer)

### AMC (Asset Management Company)

A licensed company that manages mutual funds on behalf of investors. Major Pakistani AMCs include Al-Meezan, MCB-Arif Habib, NBP Funds, UBL Funds, HBL AML, and ABL AMC.

**Example:** An investor buys units of Meezan Islamic Fund through Al-Meezan Investment Management Limited, the AMC that runs that fund.

**See also:** [Mutual fund](#mutual-fund) · [Mutual Funds page](/docs/investment-basics/mutual-funds)

### Annual report

The document published yearly by every PSX-listed company, containing audited financial statements, the directors' report, the auditor's opinion, and corporate-governance disclosures. The foundational document for fundamental analysis.

**Example:** Toyota Indus Motor's annual report for FY2025 shows revenue, profit, balance sheet items, and notes detailing every line.

**See also:** [Reading the Annual Report](/docs/fundamental-analysis/annual-report)

### Balance sheet

Officially the Statement of Financial Position. A snapshot at a single moment showing what the company owns (assets), owes (liabilities), and the residual equity. Governed by the equation Assets = Liabilities + Equity.

**Example:** A cement company's balance sheet shows PKR 50 crore in PP&E, PKR 20 crore in inventory, PKR 30 crore in long-term loans, and PKR 60 crore in equity.

**See also:** [Balance Sheet Structure](/docs/fundamental-analysis/balance-sheet) · [Liabilities](#liabilities)

### Bear market

A sustained period of declining share prices, typically defined as a drop of 20 percent or more from a recent peak. The opposite of a bull market.

**Example:** When KSE-100 fell from a peak of around 53,000 in 2017 to under 30,000 in 2020, the market was in an extended bear phase.

**See also:** [Bull market](#bull-market) · [Bull and Bear Markets](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/bull-and-bear-markets)

### Bid-ask spread

The difference between the highest price a buyer is willing to pay (the bid) and the lowest price a seller is willing to accept (the ask) for a share. A tight spread indicates a liquid, actively traded stock.

**Example:** A KSE-30 stock with a bid of PKR 199.50 and ask of PKR 200.00 has a spread of PKR 0.50, or 0.25 percent.

**See also:** [Liquidity](#liquidity) · [Order Types](/docs/trading-and-orders/order-types)

### Bonus shares

Additional shares issued free to existing shareholders in proportion to their holdings, funded from the company's reserves. The total value of the holding does not change at issuance; only the share count rises and the per-share price drops proportionally.

**Example:** A 1-for-5 bonus issue on 1,000 shares gives you 200 additional shares; if the share traded at PKR 60 before, it adjusts to roughly PKR 50 after.

**See also:** [Bonus Shares](/docs/corporate-actions/bonus-shares) · [Stock split](#stock-split)

### Book closure

The period during which a company freezes its share-transfer register so it can lock the entitlement list for a corporate action. Pakistani companies must give at least 14 days notice before book closure begins.

**Example:** ABC Ltd announces a dividend with a book closure window from 1 to 7 November; ownership recorded on the day before book closure begins is what determines who receives the dividend.

**See also:** [Book Closure](/docs/corporate-actions/book-closure) · [Record date](#record-date) · [Ex-dividend date](#ex-dividend-date)

### Book value

The accounting value of a company's equity per share, computed as total equity divided by shares outstanding. Often differs significantly from market price.

**Example:** A company with PKR 200 billion in equity and 4 billion shares has book value of PKR 50 per share; if the market price is PKR 350, the price-to-book ratio is 7.

**See also:** [Balance sheet](#balance-sheet) · [Return on Equity (ROE)](/docs/fundamental-analysis/roe)

### Bull market

A sustained period of rising share prices, typically marked by gains of 20 percent or more from a recent low and broad investor optimism. The opposite of a bear market.

**Example:** KSE-100's run from 2013 to 2017, where the index more than doubled, is widely cited as a Pakistani bull market.

**See also:** [Bear market](#bear-market) · [Bull and Bear Markets](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/bull-and-bear-markets)

### Candlestick chart

A chart format originating in 18th-century Japan that visualises four price points (open, high, low, close) for each time period as a "candle". The most-used format in modern technical analysis.

**Example:** A daily candlestick chart of a PSX stock shows green candles on days the share closed up and red candles on days it closed down, with wicks showing intraday extremes.

**See also:** [Candlestick Charts](/docs/technical-analysis/candlestick-charts) · [Technical analysis](#technical-analysis)

### Capital expenditure (capex)

Money a company spends on assets that benefit the business for multiple years (factories, machinery, vehicles), as opposed to revenue expenditure which is consumed within a year. Capex appears in the investing-activities section of the cash flow statement.

**Example:** A fertiliser company spending PKR 10 crore on new boilers expected to last 15 years is making a capex investment.

**See also:** [Reading the Annual Report](/docs/fundamental-analysis/annual-report) · [Cash Flow Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/cash-flow-statement)

### Capital gains tax (CGT)

A tax on the profit realised when you sell a share for more than you paid. In Pakistan, CGT is deducted at source by NCCPL on PSX trades, with rates that depend on holding period and filer status.

**Example:** Selling 1,000 shares for a PKR 40,000 profit triggers CGT computed on that PKR 40,000 (not the full sale proceeds).

**See also:** [Capital Gains Tax](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/capital-gains-tax) · [NCCPL](#nccpl-national-clearing-company-of-pakistan) · [Filer](#filer)

### Cash flow statement

The financial statement that restates a year's activity on a cash basis (rather than the accrual basis of the income statement), split into operating, investing, and financing activities. Often considered the hardest of the three core statements to manipulate.

**Example:** A company posting PKR 50 crore in net income but only PKR 20 crore in operating cash flow is showing profit that is being absorbed by working-capital growth or paper gains.

**See also:** [Cash Flow Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/cash-flow-statement) · [Free cash flow](#free-cash-flow-fcf)

### CDC (Central Depository Company)

The national securities depository of Pakistan, jointly regulated by SECP. Holds every PSX-listed share electronically; updates ownership records on each trade settlement.

**Example:** When you buy 100 shares of a Pakistani bank, CDC's electronic records reflect the new ownership; no paper certificate is issued.

**See also:** [CDC Account](/docs/getting-started-with-psx/cdc-account) · [CDC Sub-Account](#cdc-sub-account) · [CDC Investor Account](#cdc-investor-account-ias)

### CDC Investor Account (IAS)

A custody account opened directly with CDC, where shares can only move on the account holder's written instruction. Offers stronger protection against broker-level fraud than a Sub-Account, at the cost of slightly less convenience.

**Example:** A long-term holder transfers 5,000 shares from their broker Sub-Account to a CDC Investor Account so the broker cannot move the shares without explicit authorisation.

**See also:** [CDC Sub-Account](#cdc-sub-account) · [CDC Account page](/docs/getting-started-with-psx/cdc-account)

### CDC Sub-Account

A custody account held inside a broker's main CDC participant account. Operationally seamless for active trading but exposes the holder to broker-level risk.

**Example:** Most retail investors open a brokerage account that comes with a CDC Sub-Account by default; the broker handles settlement automatically.

**See also:** [CDC Investor Account](#cdc-investor-account-ias) · [CDC Account page](/docs/getting-started-with-psx/cdc-account)

### Circuit breaker

An automatic safeguard that halts trading when prices move sharply. PSX has stock-level daily price bands (10 percent or PKR 1, whichever is higher) and a market-wide halt triggered by a 5 percent move in the KSE-30 sustained for 5 minutes.

**Example:** A stock locked at its lower limit for the day with no buyers is said to be at "lower lock" or "lower circuit".

**See also:** [Circuit Breakers and Lower Lock](/docs/trading-and-orders/circuit-breakers)

### Compound interest

Interest earned on both the original principal and on previously accumulated interest. The mechanism behind long-term wealth building.

**Example:** PKR 1 lac invested at 12 percent compounded annually grows to roughly PKR 9.6 lacs over 20 years.

**See also:** [Power of Compounding](/docs/personal-finance/power-of-compounding)

### Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

The direct cost of producing the goods sold during a period: raw materials, direct labour, factory overhead. Subtracted from revenue to compute gross profit.

**Example:** A cement company with PKR 100 crore in revenue and PKR 60 crore in COGS has gross profit of PKR 40 crore (40 percent gross margin).

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement) · [Gross profit](#gross-profit)

### Current ratio

A liquidity ratio measuring whether a company's short-term assets cover its short-term obligations. Computed as current assets divided by current liabilities.

**Example:** A company with PKR 1 crore in current assets and PKR 80 lakh in current liabilities has a current ratio of 1.25.

**See also:** [Liquidity Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/liquidity-ratios) · [Quick ratio](#quick-ratio)

### Debt-to-equity ratio

A solvency ratio measuring how leveraged a company is. Total debt divided by total equity. Higher ratios indicate more reliance on borrowed money.

**Example:** A company with PKR 60 lakh in liabilities and PKR 100 lakh in equity has a D/E ratio of 0.6, considered moderate leverage.

**See also:** [Solvency Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/solvency-ratios) · [Leverage](#leverage)

### Diversification

Spreading investments across uncorrelated assets, sectors, or geographies to reduce overall risk without proportionally reducing return.

**Example:** Holding shares in fertiliser, banking, cement, consumer goods, and pharma instead of all in one sector reduces portfolio volatility because these sectors do not all move together.

**See also:** [Diversification](/docs/investing-101/diversification)

### Dividend

A portion of after-tax profit paid by a company to its shareholders. In Pakistan, dividends are declared as a percentage of face value (typically PKR 10), so a "300% dividend" on a PKR 10 face value is PKR 30 per share.

**Example:** Toyota Indus declares a 300 percent interim dividend; a holder of 100 shares receives PKR 3,000 (gross of withholding tax).

**See also:** [Dividends](/docs/corporate-actions/dividends) · [Dividend tax](#dividend-tax) · [Ex-dividend date](#ex-dividend-date)

### Dividend tax

The withholding tax deducted by the company before a dividend is paid to the shareholder. Pakistani rates differ by filer / late-filer / non-filer status and by sector.

**Example:** A PKR 5,000 dividend with the filer withholding rate applied results in a smaller net amount credited to the bank, with the deducted portion sent to FBR.

**See also:** [Dividend Tax](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/dividend-tax) · [Filer](#filer) · [Withholding tax](#withholding-tax)

### Dividend yield

The annual cash dividend per share divided by the current market price, expressed as a percentage. A common comparison metric across dividend-paying stocks.

**Example:** A PKR 6 annual dividend on a PKR 100 share gives a 6 percent dividend yield.

**See also:** [Dividends](/docs/corporate-actions/dividends)

### EBIT

Earnings Before Interest and Taxes. Operating profit before financing costs and tax. Used in ROCE and interest-coverage calculations because it isolates operating performance from financing decisions.

**Example:** A company with revenue PKR 100 crore, COGS PKR 60 crore, and operating expenses PKR 25 crore has EBIT of PKR 15 crore.

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement) · [Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)](#return-on-capital-employed-roce)

### EBITDA

EBIT plus depreciation and amortisation. Strips out non-cash charges in addition to financing costs and tax. Used in EV/EBITDA and other operating-comparison metrics.

**Example:** EBIT of PKR 15 crore plus PKR 5 crore depreciation gives EBITDA of PKR 20 crore.

**See also:** [Enterprise Value (EV)](/docs/fundamental-analysis/enterprise-value)

### Earnings per share (EPS)

Profit after tax divided by the weighted-average number of shares outstanding. Used as the denominator of the P/E ratio.

**Example:** A company with PKR 50 crore in PAT and 5 crore shares outstanding has EPS of PKR 10.

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement) · [P/E ratio](#pe-ratio)

### Emergency fund

A pool of cash and easily accessible savings, typically three to six months of essential expenses, set aside specifically to handle unexpected events like job loss, medical emergencies, or major repairs.

**Example:** A household spending PKR 80,000 a month on essentials should hold PKR 2.4 to 4.8 lacs in an emergency fund before investing in equities.

**See also:** [Saving and Emergency Fund](/docs/personal-finance/saving-and-emergency-fund)

### Enterprise value (EV)

The total cost of acquiring an entire company: market capitalisation plus total debt minus cash. More useful than market cap alone for comparing companies with different capital structures.

**Example:** A company with PKR 1 crore market cap, PKR 60 lakh debt, and PKR 20 lakh cash has EV of PKR 1.4 crore.

**See also:** [Enterprise Value](/docs/fundamental-analysis/enterprise-value)

### Equity

A shareholder's ownership claim on a company, equal to assets minus liabilities. Also used loosely to mean a share of common stock.

**Example:** Buying shares of Lucky Cement makes you a partial equity owner of Lucky Cement, with rights to a share of profits and votes at the AGM.

**See also:** [Stocks](/docs/investment-basics/stocks) · [Equity vs Debt](/docs/investment-basics/equity-vs-debt)

### ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)

A fund that holds a basket of securities and is itself listed on the stock exchange, traded throughout the day like a single share. Pakistani ETFs include those tracking the KSE-100 and the KMI-30.

**Example:** Buying units of a KSE-100-tracking ETF gives you exposure to all 100 index constituents in a single transaction.

**See also:** [ETFs](/docs/investment-basics/etfs) · [KSE-100](#kse-100) · [KMI-30](#kmi-30)

### Ex-dividend date

The first date on which buying a share no longer entitles you to the upcoming dividend. Under T+1 settlement, the ex-dividend date is one business day before the record date.

**Example:** A dividend with a Monday record date has its ex-dividend date on the previous Friday; buying on or after Friday means you do not qualify for the dividend.

**See also:** [Dividends](/docs/corporate-actions/dividends) · [Record date](#record-date) · [T+1 settlement](#t1-settlement)

### Face value

The par or nominal value of a share at issuance, almost always PKR 10 in Pakistan (though stock splits have brought several large names down to PKR 5, PKR 2, or PKR 1). The base for percentage-of-face-value dividend declarations.

**Example:** A "200 percent dividend" on a PKR 10 face-value share is PKR 20 per share, regardless of the current market price.

**See also:** [Dividends](/docs/corporate-actions/dividends) · [Stock split](#stock-split)

### FBR (Federal Board of Revenue)

The federal tax authority of Pakistan, responsible for income tax, sales tax, customs, and other federal taxes. Maintains the Active Taxpayer List.

**Example:** A retail investor files their annual income tax return through FBR's IRIS portal to maintain filer status.

**See also:** [Active Taxpayer List](#active-taxpayer-list-atl) · [Filer vs Non-Filer](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/filer-vs-non-filer)

### Filer

A taxpayer whose name appears on the FBR Active Taxpayer List for the relevant tax year. Filers pay lower withholding rates on most investment income.

**Example:** A filer receiving a PKR 5,000 dividend has a smaller percentage withheld than a non-filer would on the same amount.

**See also:** [Active Taxpayer List](#active-taxpayer-list-atl) · [Non-filer](#non-filer) · [Filer vs Non-Filer](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/filer-vs-non-filer)

### Free cash flow (FCF)

Operating cash flow minus capital expenditure. The cash a company has available after maintaining and growing its productive assets, used to pay dividends, repay debt, or fund acquisitions.

**Example:** A company with PKR 30 crore in operating cash flow and PKR 8 crore in capex has FCF of PKR 22 crore.

**See also:** [Cash Flow Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/cash-flow-statement)

### Free-float

The number of shares of a company actually available for trading, excluding strategic holdings by sponsors, government, and locked-in shares. PSX indices use free-float market capitalisation rather than total market cap.

**Example:** A company with 1 billion shares total but 700 million locked in family ownership has 300 million free-float shares; index weighting reflects only the latter.

**See also:** [PSX Indices](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/psx-indices) · [Market capitalisation](#market-capitalisation)

### Fundamental analysis

The discipline of determining a stock's intrinsic value by examining financial statements, management quality, the company's moat, and the broader economic and industry context. Compared to technical analysis, fundamental analysis suits long-term investing.

**Example:** A fundamental analyst studying a Pakistani fertiliser company reads its annual reports, computes ratios, and compares against industry peers before estimating a fair value range.

**See also:** [Fundamental Analysis category](/docs/category/fundamental-analysis) · [Technical analysis](#technical-analysis) · [Intrinsic value](#intrinsic-value)

### Gharar

In Islamic finance, excessive uncertainty in contracts. Conventional derivatives, speculative options, and certain insurance structures are considered to involve gharar and are therefore non-compliant.

**Example:** A conventional futures contract on a commodity is generally classified as gharar-based; takaful insurance structured to avoid gharar is the compliant alternative.

**See also:** [What Is Shariah Investing?](/docs/shariah-investing/what-is-shariah-investing) · [Riba](#riba)

### Going concern

The accounting assumption that a company will continue operating indefinitely into the foreseeable future, allowing it to value long-term assets at operating value rather than liquidation value. Auditors issue qualifications when this assumption is in doubt.

**Example:** An auditor's report flagging "material uncertainty regarding going concern" signals that the company may not survive 12 months.

**See also:** [Accounting Principles](/docs/fundamental-analysis/accounting-principles) · [The Independent Auditor Report](/docs/fundamental-analysis/auditor-report)

### Good-till-cancelled (GTC)

An order validity that keeps the order active until it executes or is cancelled, rather than expiring at end of day. Useful for price-disciplined orders that may take days to fill.

**Example:** A GTC limit buy order at PKR 95 on a stock currently trading at PKR 100 sits in the order book until the stock falls to PKR 95 or you cancel.

**See also:** [Order Types](/docs/trading-and-orders/order-types) · [Limit order](#limit-order)

### Gross profit

Revenue minus cost of goods sold. The profit margin on the core production activity, before any selling, administrative, or financing costs.

**Example:** Revenue of PKR 100 crore minus COGS of PKR 60 crore equals gross profit of PKR 40 crore (40 percent gross margin).

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement) · [COGS](#cost-of-goods-sold-cogs)

### Hawl

In Islamic jurisprudence, the lunar-year holding requirement for zakat. Zakat becomes due once one full lunar year has passed on assets above the nisab threshold.

**Example:** A Pakistani investor whose holdings exceeded nisab on 1 Ramadan last year computes zakat on the holdings still above nisab on 1 Ramadan this year.

**See also:** [Zakat on Stocks](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/zakat-on-stocks) · [Nisab](#nisab) · [Zakat](#zakat)

### Income statement

Officially the Statement of Profit or Loss. Shows revenue and expenses over a period, ending in net income (PAT). Walks from revenue down through COGS, operating expenses, finance cost, and tax.

**Example:** A textile company's income statement for FY2025 shows revenue PKR 50 crore, COGS PKR 30 crore, operating expenses PKR 10 crore, finance cost PKR 3 crore, tax PKR 2 crore, and PAT PKR 5 crore.

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement)

### Intrinsic value

The estimated true worth of a share based on the underlying business's fundamentals. The fundamental investor's central concept: buy when intrinsic value is meaningfully above market price.

**Example:** An analyst estimates a Pakistani consumer-goods company's intrinsic value at PKR 500 to PKR 600 per share; the stock currently trades at PKR 380, suggesting an undervalued opportunity.

**See also:** [Intrinsic Value vs Market Price](/docs/fundamental-analysis/intrinsic-value-vs-market-price)

### IPO (Initial Public Offering)

The first sale of shares by a company to the public, listing the company on the stock exchange. The transition from private ownership to public ownership.

**Example:** A Pakistani technology company raising fresh capital through an IPO on PSX issues new shares at a fixed offer price set in consultation with SECP.

**See also:** [Primary vs Secondary Market](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/primary-vs-secondary-market)

### KATS (Karachi Automated Trading System)

PSX's electronic order-matching system that handles every PSX trade. Replaces the old physical trading floor.

**Example:** When you press buy on your broker app, the order travels through the broker's system to KATS, which matches it with an opposite sell order in milliseconds.

**See also:** [How PSX Works](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/how-psx-works)

### KIBOR (Karachi Interbank Offered Rate)

The benchmark interest rate at which Pakistani banks lend to each other. Most floating-rate Pakistani loans are priced as KIBOR plus a spread.

**Example:** A long-term corporate loan priced at "3-month KIBOR plus 3 percent" with KIBOR at 12 percent carries a total rate of 15 percent.

**See also:** [Interest Coverage Ratio](/docs/fundamental-analysis/interest-coverage-ratio)

### KMI-30

The KSE Meezan Index 30, Pakistan's primary Shariah-compliant equity benchmark. Tracks the 30 most liquid Shariah-compliant companies on PSX, free-float weighted with a 12 percent individual cap, re-composed semi-annually on 30 June and 31 December.

**Example:** A Pakistani Muslim investor benchmarks their Shariah-compliant equity fund's performance against the KMI-30.

**See also:** [KMI-30 Index](/docs/shariah-investing/kmi-30-index) · [Shariah Screening](/docs/shariah-investing/shariah-screening)

### KMI All Share Index

The broader Shariah-compliant index covering every Shariah-compliant company on PSX, not just the largest 30. Methodology approved by SECP in May 2024.

**Example:** A Shariah-compliant fund seeking exposure to small and mid-cap names benchmarks against the PSX-KMI All Share Index rather than the narrower KMI-30.

**See also:** [KMI-30](#kmi-30) · [Shariah Investing category](/docs/category/shariah-investing)

### KSE-30

The narrower of PSX's two main free-float-weighted indices, tracking 30 of the most liquid PSX-listed companies. Used as the trigger reference for the market-wide circuit-breaker.

**Example:** A 5 percent move in the KSE-30 sustained for 5 minutes triggers a 45-minute market-wide trading halt outside the last hour of trading.

**See also:** [PSX Indices](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/psx-indices) · [Circuit Breakers](/docs/trading-and-orders/circuit-breakers)

### KSE-100

The headline PSX index, tracking 100 companies (the largest in each sector plus other major names) using free-float market-capitalisation weighting. The most-quoted single number for "the Pakistani stock market".

**Example:** "KSE-100 closed up 1.2 percent today" describes a one-day rise in the broad index, computed across the 100 constituents.

**See also:** [PSX Indices](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/psx-indices) · [Free-float](#free-float)

### Late filer

An intermediate category recently introduced in some Finance Acts, sitting between filer and non-filer for withholding-tax purposes. Applied to taxpayers who file but after the original deadline.

**Example:** A taxpayer filing for tax year 2025 after the September deadline but within the late-filing window pays withholding rates between filer and non-filer levels until the late-filer status is updated.

**See also:** [Filer](#filer) · [Non-filer](#non-filer) · [Filer vs Non-Filer](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/filer-vs-non-filer)

### Leverage

Using borrowed money to amplify returns (or losses). At the company level, leverage is measured by debt-to-equity. At the trader level, leverage refers to margin or borrowed funds for trading.

**Example:** A company with PKR 100 crore in equity and PKR 200 crore in debt has 2:1 leverage; if the underlying business returns 10 percent, equity holders earn more than 10 percent because debt is fixed-cost.

**See also:** [Solvency Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/solvency-ratios) · [Debt-to-equity ratio](#debt-to-equity-ratio)

### Liabilities

What a company owes to others: bank loans, supplier payables, accrued expenses, deferred items, employee obligations. Split into current (due within 12 months) and non-current.

**Example:** A balance sheet line "Long-term loans PKR 50 crore" represents debt repayable beyond the next 12 months.

**See also:** [Liabilities](/docs/fundamental-analysis/liabilities) · [Balance sheet](#balance-sheet)

### Limit order

An order to buy or sell only at a specific price or better. Trades discipline for execution certainty: the order may or may not fill, depending on whether the market reaches your price.

**Example:** A buy limit order at PKR 95 on a stock trading at PKR 100 fills only if the stock falls to PKR 95 or lower.

**See also:** [Order Types](/docs/trading-and-orders/order-types) · [Market order](#market-order)

### Liquidity

The ease with which an asset can be converted to cash without significantly affecting its price. A liquid stock has tight bid-ask spreads and substantial daily volume; an illiquid stock has wide spreads and thin trading.

**Example:** KSE-30 constituents are highly liquid; small-cap PSX names trading less than PKR 1 lac per day are illiquid.

**See also:** [Liquidity Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/liquidity-ratios) · [Bid-ask spread](#bid-ask-spread)

### Lower lock

When a stock hits its daily lower price band (typically minus 10 percent or PKR 1, whichever is higher) with sellers stacked up but no buyers willing to bid at the lock price. Trading at the lock continues only if a buyer appears.

**Example:** A bad-news day on a Pakistani cement stock can lock it at the lower limit for the entire session, with no fills available.

**See also:** [Circuit Breakers and Lower Lock](/docs/trading-and-orders/circuit-breakers) · [Upper lock](#upper-lock)

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

A trend-and-momentum technical indicator built from exponential moving averages. The MACD line crossing above its signal line is interpreted as bullish; crossing below is bearish.

**Example:** A trader watching a daily PSX chart sees the MACD line cross above the signal line and reads it as a short-term bullish signal.

**See also:** [Technical Indicators](/docs/technical-analysis/technical-indicators) · [Moving average](#moving-average)

### Market capitalisation

The total market value of a company's outstanding shares, computed as current share price times shares outstanding. The basic measure of company size.

**Example:** A company with 1 crore shares outstanding at PKR 500 each has market cap of PKR 500 crore.

**See also:** [PSX Indices](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/psx-indices) · [Free-float](#free-float)

### Market order

An order to buy or sell immediately at the best available price. Trades execution certainty for some price slippage on illiquid stocks.

**Example:** A market buy order on a liquid PSX stock fills within seconds at or near the current ask price.

**See also:** [Order Types](/docs/trading-and-orders/order-types) · [Limit order](#limit-order)

### Moat

A structural advantage that protects a company from competitors over the long term: cost advantage, network effect, switching costs, brand, distribution, or regulatory barrier. Buffett's term, drawn from castle defences.

**Example:** Suzuki Pakistan's decades-old dealer and after-sales network is a distribution moat that has helped it withstand competition from Chery, Hyundai, and Daihatsu.

**See also:** [Qualitative Analysis](/docs/fundamental-analysis/qualitative-analysis)

### Moving average

A technical indicator that averages price over a set number of recent periods, smoothing short-term noise to reveal the underlying trend. Simple (SMA) and exponential (EMA) variants are most common.

**Example:** The 200-day SMA of a PSX stock is the average of its last 200 daily closing prices, used as a long-term trend reference.

**See also:** [Technical Indicators](/docs/technical-analysis/technical-indicators)

### Mutual fund

A pooled investment vehicle managed by a licensed AMC that invests in a diversified portfolio of securities on behalf of unit holders. Pakistani mutual funds cover equity, fixed-income, money market, Shariah-compliant, and balanced strategies.

**Example:** A retail investor buys 10,000 units of Meezan Islamic Fund through Al-Meezan Investment Management, gaining diversified Shariah-compliant exposure.

**See also:** [Mutual Funds](/docs/investment-basics/mutual-funds) · [AMC](#amc-asset-management-company) · [NAV](#net-asset-value-nav)

### NCCPL (National Clearing Company of Pakistan)

The central counterparty for PSX trades and the entity that computes and deducts capital gains tax at source. Sits between every buyer and every seller, guaranteeing both legs of the trade.

**Example:** A PSX share sale settled on T+1 has cash and shares moving through NCCPL and CDC respectively; CGT is deducted by NCCPL before proceeds reach the broker.

**See also:** [Settlement](/docs/trading-and-orders/settlement) · [Capital Gains Tax](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/capital-gains-tax) · [CDC](#cdc-central-depository-company)

### Net asset value (NAV)

The per-unit value of a mutual fund, computed as the fund's total assets minus liabilities, divided by units outstanding. Updated daily.

**Example:** A fund with PKR 100 crore in assets minus PKR 1 crore in liabilities and 1 crore units outstanding has NAV of PKR 99 per unit.

**See also:** [Mutual Funds](/docs/investment-basics/mutual-funds)

### Net worth

The difference between total assets and total liabilities, applied either to a person or a company. The single most useful number for tracking personal financial progress.

**Example:** Personal assets of PKR 50 lacs minus liabilities of PKR 20 lacs equals net worth of PKR 30 lacs.

**See also:** [Net Worth](/docs/personal-finance/net-worth)

### Nisab

In Islamic jurisprudence, the minimum threshold of wealth above which zakat is owed. Set in fiqh based on a fixed weight of gold (87.48g) or silver (612.36g), with the PKR equivalent recalculated yearly.

**Example:** Pakistani Shariah boards publish the silver-standard nisab annually, often around Ramadan; if a household's combined wealth is below that figure, no zakat is owed for the year.

**See also:** [Zakat on Stocks](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/zakat-on-stocks) · [Hawl](#hawl) · [Zakat](#zakat)

### Non-filer

A taxpayer whose name does not appear on the FBR Active Taxpayer List. Pays higher withholding rates on most investment income.

**Example:** A non-filer receiving a PKR 5,000 dividend has a meaningfully larger percentage withheld than a filer would; the differential is the strongest argument for filing.

**See also:** [Filer](#filer) · [Active Taxpayer List](#active-taxpayer-list-atl) · [Filer vs Non-Filer](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/filer-vs-non-filer)

### PAT (Profit After Tax)

Net income. The bottom line of the income statement, after subtracting all expenses, finance cost, and taxes from revenue.

**Example:** Revenue PKR 100 crore, all expenses including tax PKR 88 crore, gives PAT of PKR 12 crore.

**See also:** [Income Statement](/docs/fundamental-analysis/income-statement) · [Earnings per share (EPS)](#earnings-per-share-eps)

### Payout ratio

The percentage of earnings paid out as dividends, computed as dividend per share divided by EPS. The complement is the plowback ratio.

**Example:** A company with EPS of PKR 50 paying a PKR 20 dividend has a 40 percent payout ratio and a 60 percent plowback ratio.

**See also:** [Payout and Plowback Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/payout-and-plowback) · [Plowback ratio](#plowback-ratio)

### P/E ratio

Price-to-earnings ratio. Current share price divided by earnings per share. Tells you how much the market is paying for each rupee of annual earnings.

**Example:** A PSX stock trading at PKR 200 with EPS of PKR 10 has a P/E of 20; whether that is high or low depends on industry and growth.

**See also:** [P/E Ratio](/docs/fundamental-analysis/pe-ratio) · [Earnings per share (EPS)](#earnings-per-share-eps)

### Plowback ratio

The percentage of earnings retained inside the business rather than paid out as dividends. The complement of the payout ratio.

**Example:** A company paying out 40 percent of EPS as dividends has a 60 percent plowback ratio; whether that retention creates value depends on the company's reinvestment ROCE.

**See also:** [Payout and Plowback Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/payout-and-plowback) · [Payout ratio](#payout-ratio)

### Pre-open session

A 15-minute session at PSX (09:15 to 09:30 AM on normal days) before regular trading begins, during which orders can be placed and modified but not matched. Orders queued during this session contribute to the opening price.

**Example:** A buy order placed at 09:20 AM goes into the pre-open book; it does not execute until 09:30 AM when matching begins.

**See also:** [Trading Hours](/docs/trading-and-orders/trading-hours)

### Primary market

The market in which new shares are issued directly by companies to investors, typically through an IPO. Distinct from the secondary market where existing shares trade between investors.

**Example:** A Pakistani company raising fresh capital through an IPO is participating in the primary market.

**See also:** [Primary vs Secondary Market](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/primary-vs-secondary-market) · [IPO](#ipo-initial-public-offering) · [Secondary market](#secondary-market)

### Property, Plant and Equipment (PP&E)

The largest non-current asset for most operating companies: factories, machinery, vehicles, computers, furniture, and similar long-lived tangible assets.

**Example:** A cement manufacturer's PP&E includes the cement mill, kiln, conveyors, and packing plant, depreciated over their useful lives.

**See also:** [Non-Current Assets](/docs/fundamental-analysis/non-current-assets)

### Purification

The donation to charity of the non-compliant income portion of dividends from otherwise-Shariah-compliant companies. Doctrinally distinct from zakat; donor receives no spiritual reward.

**Example:** A 3 percent non-compliant income ratio applied to a PKR 5,000 dividend gives PKR 150 to be donated as purification.

**See also:** [Purification](/docs/shariah-investing/purification) · [Shariah Screening](/docs/shariah-investing/shariah-screening) · [Zakat](#zakat)

### Quick ratio

A liquidity ratio stricter than the current ratio. Excludes inventory from current assets because inventory cannot always be quickly converted to cash. Also called the acid-test ratio.

**Example:** A company with PKR 1 crore current assets, PKR 30 lakh inventory, and PKR 80 lakh current liabilities has a quick ratio of 0.875.

**See also:** [Liquidity Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/liquidity-ratios) · [Current ratio](#current-ratio)

### Record date

The date on which a company captures its share register to determine which shareholders are entitled to an upcoming dividend, bonus, rights, or other corporate action.

**Example:** A dividend announcement specifying a record date of 31 October means whoever is on the share register on that date receives the dividend.

**See also:** [Book Closure](/docs/corporate-actions/book-closure) · [Ex-dividend date](#ex-dividend-date)

### Return on Assets (ROA)

A profitability ratio measuring how efficiently a company uses its total assets to generate profit. PAT divided by total assets.

**Example:** A textile company with PAT of PKR 1 crore and total assets of PKR 5 crore has ROA of 20 percent.

**See also:** [Return on Assets (ROA)](/docs/fundamental-analysis/roa)

### Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)

EBIT divided by capital employed (typically equity plus debt). A cleaner cross-company efficiency measure than ROE because it removes the leverage distortion.

**Example:** A company with EBIT of PKR 28 lakh and capital employed of PKR 1 crore has ROCE of 28 percent.

**See also:** [ROCE](/docs/fundamental-analysis/roce)

### Return on Equity (ROE)

Buffett's favourite efficiency ratio. Net income divided by shareholders' equity, expressed as a percentage. Measures how well management uses shareholders' money to generate profit.

**Example:** A bank with PKR 8 crore PAT and PKR 40 crore equity has ROE of 20 percent.

**See also:** [Return on Equity (ROE)](/docs/fundamental-analysis/roe)

### Riba

In Islamic finance, interest or unlawful gain. Prohibited in all forms; the foundational reason conventional banking and interest-bearing debt are non-compliant.

**Example:** A conventional savings account paying interest is riba-based; an Islamic-banking-equivalent account using mudarabah profit-sharing is the compliant alternative.

**See also:** [What Is Shariah Investing?](/docs/shariah-investing/what-is-shariah-investing) · [Gharar](#gharar)

### Rights issue

An offer by a company to existing shareholders to buy additional shares at a discounted price, in proportion to their current holdings. Distinct from a bonus issue (which is free).

**Example:** A 1-for-4 rights issue at PKR 25 on a stock trading at PKR 60 lets a shareholder with 1,000 shares buy 250 new shares at PKR 25 each.

**See also:** [Rights Issues](/docs/corporate-actions/rights-issues) · [Bonus shares](#bonus-shares)

### Risk

The chance that an investment will produce a return different from what was expected, particularly a worse one. Typically measured by volatility (standard deviation of returns), drawdown, or qualitative judgement.

**Example:** Pakistani small-cap equities have higher volatility (and therefore higher risk by that measure) than KSE-30 blue chips.

**See also:** [Risk and Return](/docs/investing-101/risk-and-return) · [Types of Risk](/docs/investing-101/types-of-risk)

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)

A momentum technical indicator measuring the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. Default 14-period; ranges 0 to 100. Above 70 is conventionally overbought; below 30 is oversold.

**Example:** A PSX stock that has rallied 20 percent in two weeks may show RSI above 70, suggesting short-term overextension.

**See also:** [Technical Indicators](/docs/technical-analysis/technical-indicators)

### Sahulat Account

A simplified PSX trading account introduced by SECP and PSX to lower the barrier for first-time retail investors. Requires only a CNIC for KYC, capped at Rs. 3 million in total holdings, regular-market access only.

**Example:** A first-time investor with PKR 50,000 to invest opens a Sahulat Account at a participating broker using just their CNIC.

**See also:** [Opening an Account](/docs/getting-started-with-psx/opening-an-account)

### Secondary market

The market in which existing shares trade between investors after their initial issuance. PSX itself is a secondary market.

**Example:** Buying 100 shares of Lucky Cement on PSX from another investor (not from Lucky Cement directly) is a secondary-market transaction.

**See also:** [Primary vs Secondary Market](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/primary-vs-secondary-market) · [Primary market](#primary-market)

### SECP (Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan)

The federal regulator for capital markets, mutual funds, brokers, and listed companies in Pakistan. Roughly equivalent to the SEC in the US.

**Example:** A new IPO must obtain SECP approval before listing on PSX; mutual funds register with and are supervised by SECP.

**See also:** [Regulations and Ethics](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/regulations-and-ethics) · [Market Participants](/docs/pakistan-stock-market/market-participants)

### Settlement

The actual transfer of cash and shares to fulfil a trade. Pakistani equities settle on T+1, meaning one business day after the trade.

**Example:** A Monday trade settles on Tuesday; on the settlement day, shares move into the buyer's CDC account and cash moves to the seller through NCCPL.

**See also:** [Settlement](/docs/trading-and-orders/settlement) · [T+1 settlement](#t1-settlement) · [NCCPL](#nccpl-national-clearing-company-of-pakistan)

### Shariah-compliant fund

A mutual fund whose holdings are screened against Shariah principles by a Shariah advisory board, with non-compliant income purified and reported per unit. Major Pakistani Shariah-compliant funds include Meezan Islamic Fund, Al-Meezan, NBP Islamic, and UBL Islamic.

**Example:** An investor buying units of Meezan Islamic Fund relies on Al-Meezan's Shariah board to maintain compliance and publish per-unit purification.

**See also:** [What Is Shariah Investing?](/docs/shariah-investing/what-is-shariah-investing) · [Shariah Screening](/docs/shariah-investing/shariah-screening)

### Solvency

The long-term ability of a company to meet its total obligations, distinct from short-term liquidity. Measured by debt-to-equity, debt-to-assets, and interest coverage ratios.

**Example:** A company with debt-to-assets above 1 is technically insolvent: total liabilities exceed total assets.

**See also:** [Solvency Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/solvency-ratios) · [Liquidity](#liquidity)

### Stock split

A corporate action that reduces a share's face value and issues additional shares in proportion, leaving the total holding value unchanged. Pakistani examples include AHCL (10:1), Lucky Cement and Systems Limited (5:1), and National Foods (2:1).

**Example:** A 5-for-1 split on a PKR 1,000 share with face value PKR 10 produces 5 new shares at roughly PKR 200 each, with face value PKR 2.

**See also:** [Stock Splits](/docs/corporate-actions/stock-splits) · [Bonus shares](#bonus-shares)

### Stop-loss order

An order that triggers a market sell when the share price falls to a defined level, used to limit losses on a position.

**Example:** A holder of a stock at PKR 100 places a stop-loss at PKR 90; if the price falls to PKR 90, the stop converts to a market sell.

**See also:** [Order Types](/docs/trading-and-orders/order-types)

### Sukuk

Islamic finance certificates that represent ownership of underlying assets and share in their economic returns, structured to avoid riba. The Shariah-compliant alternative to conventional bonds.

**Example:** Pakistan has issued sovereign sukuk in PKR and USD; corporate sukuk are issued by Islamic-banking-aligned Pakistani companies.

**See also:** [Bonds](/docs/investment-basics/bonds) · [Riba](#riba)

### Support and resistance

In technical analysis, price levels at which buying interest (support) or selling interest (resistance) has historically been strong enough to halt or reverse moves. Once broken, support often becomes resistance and vice versa.

**Example:** A PSX stock that has bounced off PKR 95 multiple times has support at PKR 95; a clear break below would often be interpreted as a bearish signal.

**See also:** [Chart Patterns](/docs/technical-analysis/chart-patterns)

### T+1 settlement

The Pakistani equity settlement cycle effective 9 February 2026 (replacing the previous T+2). Trades match on day T and settle on the next business day, with cash and shares actually moving between accounts on T+1.

**Example:** A Monday share purchase has cash deducted and shares delivered on Tuesday; if there is a Tuesday holiday, settlement slides to Wednesday.

**See also:** [Settlement](/docs/trading-and-orders/settlement) · [NCCPL](#nccpl-national-clearing-company-of-pakistan)

### Takaful

The Islamic alternative to conventional insurance. Structured as a mutual cooperative arrangement to share risk, avoiding gharar and riba.

**Example:** A Pakistani Muslim investor uses a takaful policy from one of several Islamic insurers rather than a conventional insurance contract.

**See also:** [What Is Shariah Investing?](/docs/shariah-investing/what-is-shariah-investing) · [Gharar](#gharar)

### Technical analysis

The discipline of analysing share-price charts, patterns, and indicators to forecast short-term price movements, in contrast to fundamental analysis which studies the underlying business.

**Example:** A trader uses moving averages, RSI, and chart patterns on a daily PSX chart to identify short-term entry and exit signals.

**See also:** [Technical Analysis category](/docs/category/technical-analysis) · [Fundamental analysis](#fundamental-analysis)

### Time value of money

The principle that a rupee today is worth more than a rupee tomorrow because the rupee today can be invested and grown. The mathematical foundation of valuation, dividend discount models, and any forward-looking financial calculation.

**Example:** PKR 1 lac received today is more valuable than PKR 1 lac received in 5 years because the present amount can be invested at, say, 12 percent for 5 years.

**See also:** [Time Value of Money](/docs/personal-finance/time-value-of-money) · [Compound interest](#compound-interest)

### TREC (Trading Right Entitlement Certificate)

The licence required for a brokerage firm to trade on PSX. Issued by SECP and PSX jointly.

**Example:** Verifying a Pakistani broker's SECP TREC status is the first step in evaluating whether the broker is legally authorised to operate.

**See also:** [Choosing a Broker](/docs/getting-started-with-psx/choosing-a-broker) · [SECP](#secp-securities-and-exchange-commission-of-pakistan)

### Trendline

A diagonal line connecting a series of higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs (downtrend) on a price chart. A clear break of a long-standing trendline is often interpreted as a signal that the trend is reversing.

**Example:** A daily PSX chart with a rising trendline connecting six monthly lows over a year defines a long-standing uptrend; a clear daily break below would signal possible reversal.

**See also:** [Chart Patterns](/docs/technical-analysis/chart-patterns)

### Upper lock

When a stock hits its daily upper price band (typically plus 10 percent or PKR 1, whichever is higher) with buyers stacked up but no sellers willing to offer at the lock price.

**Example:** A blockbuster earnings surprise on a small-cap PSX stock can lock it at the upper limit for the entire session.

**See also:** [Circuit Breakers and Lower Lock](/docs/trading-and-orders/circuit-breakers) · [Lower lock](#lower-lock)

### Volatility

The degree to which an asset's price fluctuates over time. Higher volatility means larger swings (in either direction); often used as a proxy for risk.

**Example:** Pakistani small-cap stocks typically have higher annual volatility than KSE-30 blue chips, sometimes by a factor of two to three.

**See also:** [Risk and Return](/docs/investing-101/risk-and-return)

### Volume

The number of shares traded in a security over a defined period. A core technical-analysis input; price moves on heavy volume are typically considered more meaningful than the same moves on thin volume.

**Example:** A PSX stock that breaks above its prior high on twice the average daily volume is showing a stronger breakout signal than the same break on half the average volume.

**See also:** [Technical Indicators](/docs/technical-analysis/technical-indicators)

### Withholding tax

A tax deducted at source by the payer (company or broker) before the recipient receives the payment. Most Pakistani investment-income taxation works through withholding.

**Example:** A PKR 5,000 dividend has the applicable withholding rate deducted by the company before the net amount is credited to the shareholder's bank.

**See also:** [Dividend Tax](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/dividend-tax) · [Capital Gains Tax](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/capital-gains-tax)

### Working capital

Current assets minus current liabilities. The day-to-day operating capital available to fund inventory, receivables, and short-term obligations.

**Example:** A company with PKR 1 crore current assets and PKR 80 lakh current liabilities has PKR 20 lakh in working capital.

**See also:** [Liquidity Ratios](/docs/fundamental-analysis/liquidity-ratios) · [Balance sheet](#balance-sheet)

### Yield

The income return on an investment, typically expressed as an annual percentage of the price. Includes dividend yield (for stocks), coupon yield (for bonds), and rental yield (for real estate).

**Example:** A Pakistani share trading at PKR 100 paying an annual dividend of PKR 8 has a dividend yield of 8 percent.

**See also:** [Dividend yield](#dividend-yield)

### Zakat

A pillar of Islam: an annual religious obligation to donate 2.5 percent of zakatable wealth above the nisab threshold, after one full lunar year of holding. Calculated on cash, gold, silver, business stock, and (under most opinions) the market value of trading shares.

**Example:** A Pakistani Muslim with PKR 10 lacs in zakatable wealth above nisab on 1 Ramadan owes PKR 25,000 in zakat for the year.

**See also:** [Zakat on Stocks](/docs/taxes-and-zakat/zakat-on-stocks) · [Nisab](#nisab) · [Hawl](#hawl) · [Purification](#purification)
