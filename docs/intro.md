# Welcome

Three numbers worth sitting with for a moment. Over the past year, the [KSE-100](https://tradingeconomics.com/pakistan/stock-market), the main index of the Pakistan Stock Exchange, returned more than 50 percent. Pakistani [inflation](https://www.brecorder.com/news/40419167) over the same period ran at around 11 percent. A typical bank savings account paid single-digit returns.

In plain language, money sitting in a savings account has been losing real value every month, while the same money sitting in a basket of PSX-listed companies has been multiplying. Most Pakistanis have never seriously considered the stock market because they assume it is gambling, or that you need lacs of rupees to start, or that you need to be a finance professional to make sense of it. None of that is true.

This guide is the on-ramp. Open an account at a registered PSX broker for [around Rs. 5,000](https://www.psx.com.pk/psx/resources-and-tools/investors/open-an-account-invest), learn the rules from this material over a few weeks, start small, and stay patient. Everything beyond that is mechanics, and that is exactly what every page from here on teaches.

## Start here

Open [What is investing?](/docs/investing-101/what-is-investing) next. It is a short read, the foundation everything else builds on, and by the time you finish it you will know whether the stock market is worth your time.

If your finances are not yet in order (no emergency fund, inconsistent monthly savings, debt you have not addressed), take one step back to [Personal Finance](/docs/category/personal-finance) first. Investing rewards people whose basic money habits are already in place.

<Callout type="tip" title="One page at a time">
  You do not have to read everything in a weekend. Reading one page each evening over a few weeks will leave you better prepared to invest than the vast majority of people opening their first PSX account.
</Callout>

## What you will find here

Twelve sections take you from the foundations of personal money management to reading a company's annual report and judging whether its stock is worth buying:

- **Personal Finance**: budgeting, saving, the power of compounding, the time value of money.
- **Investing 101**: what investing actually is, why it matters, how to think about risk and diversification.
- **Investment Basics**: stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, equity vs. debt.
- **Pakistan Stock Market**: how PSX works, KSE-100 and KMI-30, the participants (brokers, CDC, NCCPL, SECP).
- **Getting Started with PSX**: opening an account, choosing a broker, placing your first trade.
- **Trading and Orders**: order types, trading hours, T+1 settlement, circuit breakers.
- **Corporate Actions**: dividends, bonus shares, stock splits, rights issues, book closure.
- **Fundamental Analysis**: reading financial statements and the ratios that tell you whether a company is worth buying.
- **Technical Analysis**: chart reading basics for short term price movements.
- **Taxes and Zakat**: capital gains, dividend tax, filer vs. non-filer status, zakat on stocks.
- **Shariah Investing**: KMI-30, Shariah screening, purification of non-compliant income.
- **Glossary**: a quick reference for the PSX and investing terms you will encounter.

## How each page is laid out

Every page follows the same structure. A short introduction frames why the topic matters in Pakistan. A "What you'll learn" list previews what is coming. Core concepts are explained in plain language. A worked example uses PKR amounts where it helps. Key takeaways close the page, with a disclaimer wherever the topic touches taxes, zakat, or specific securities. The search box at the top of every page helps when you already know what you are looking for.
